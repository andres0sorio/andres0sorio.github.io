Senior Software & Infrastructure Engineer · Scientific Computing Background

I am a senior engineer with a background in experimental physics, software development, and infrastructure engineering, with experience spanning scientific research, data-intensive systems, and production infrastructure.

My career began in high-energy physics (CERN / CMS), working on large-scale distributed systems and data analysis. Over time, I transitioned toward software and infrastructure engineering, where I have focused on deploying, operating, and supporting complex systems in real organizational contexts.

This GitHub profile collects selected public work, experiments, and technical explorations across those domains.

Scientific & Research Work

CMS Experiment – CERN (2008–2013)

During my time at CERN, I worked within the CMS experiment, contributing to software and analysis workflows supporting trigger systems and detector data processing.

  • Data analysis and validation code
  • Emulation and testing of trigger components (RPC Technical Trigger Unit or TTU)
  • Experimental C++ and Python tools developed in a large, distributed collaboration

This period shaped my approach to distributed systems under strict correctness constraints, reproducibility, and engineering in high-stakes environments.

High-Energy Neutrinos & Astrophysics (Universidad de los Andes)

While studying high-energy neutrinos, I developed numerical models for neutrino oscillations in matter with varying density, extending prior work by Tommy Ohlsson.

This work resulted in peer-reviewed publications:

  • Oscillation of high energy neutrinos in choked GRBs
    Eur. Phys. J. C73 (2013) 2574
    DOI: 10.1140/epjc/s10052-013-2574-8
  • High energy neutrinos from choked GRBs and their flavor ratio measurement by IceCube
    Eur. Phys. J. C75 (2015) 289
    DOI: 10.1140/epjc/s10052-015-3524-4

This experience continues to inform how I think about modeling, validation, and system behavior under uncertainty.

Infrastructure & Applied Engineering

Infrastructure & Deployment Work (C1)

At C1, I worked on infrastructure, deployment efforts, the use and application of best practices with a focus on operationalizing complex software systems rather than pure feature development.

A key project was the deployment of Open edX for a pilot education program, which involved:

  • Deploying and operating Open edX on Kubernetes
  • Defining and provisioning supporting infrastructure components
  • Working across application, infrastructure, and operational boundaries
  • Supporting a real pilot with production-like constraints
  • Monitoring and maintaining the deployed systems

This work required practical experience with Kubernetes fundamentals, infrastructure setup and lifecycle management, best practices, and troubleshooting distributed systems in live environments.

While recent, this experience represents hands-on infrastructure work in a real delivery context, not just experimentation.
